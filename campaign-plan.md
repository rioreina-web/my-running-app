# Post Run Drip — Launch Campaign Plan

**Date authored:** 2026-04-20
**Budget:** ~$200–$400 total (everything else is relationship leverage)
**Timeline:** 2 weeks pre-launch → launch week → 6 weeks post-launch (~9 weeks)

---

## 1. The premise

**You have something most seed-stage founders don't: access.** You're already in Austin running circles, already running the Heat Check Mile, already know coaches, athletes, and local run-club leaders. That network is worth more than $50K of paid media at this stage. The entire campaign should be built on leverage, not spend.

**One rule:** if a tactic requires money, ask first *"can I get this through a relationship instead?"* Almost always the answer is yes.

---

## 2. The objective

**Get to 300–500 active users in 60 days, all from warm channels.** If you're spending money to acquire users at this stage, the product isn't the problem — distribution is, and you're not solving it the right way.

Sub-goals:
- 50 users by end of launch week (pure network)
- 200 by end of month 1 (network + early UGC)
- 400–500 by end of month 2 (compound via referrals + earned media)
- 5+ podcast/creator features earned, not paid
- 1,000+ Instagram followers from baseline

---

## 3. Your network inventory — do this first

Before any content, before any posts, before any launch announcement: **make a spreadsheet of every running connection you have and what each one can do.** Not "who would I spam" — who would genuinely share this if you asked.

Columns:

| Name | Relationship | Audience / platform | Size | What they could do | Ask date | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Categories to fill in:

- **Elite/sub-elite runners you know personally** — people with 10K+ IG followers who'd post a genuine reaction to the app
- **Local run club leaders** (Rogue, ARC, Gilbert's, Austin Distance Project, etc.) — they have audiences who listen
- **Running coaches in your Rolodex** — people whose word carries
- **Running podcast hosts you've met** — Ali Feller, Dylan Bowman, David Roche, whoever you've actually crossed paths with
- **Sports scientists / writers** — people who write about training, not people who post workout photos
- **Heat Check Mile past participants** — your single most warm list of users
- **Friends who run** — the least glamorous but highest-conversion tier. A personal "hey you'd actually like this" DM to 30 friends is worth more than a million IG impressions.

**Target: 50 names.** Not 500. 50 specific people who each have a specific thing they could do.

If you can get this list to 50 before launch week, the rest of the plan is mostly scheduling.

## 4. Pre-launch — 2 weeks (April 20 – May 1)

### Week 1 — Quiet prep

**No public posts yet.** This is preparation.

- **Build the network list above.** Goal: 50 names with specific asks.
- **Closed beta via personal DMs.** Send 15–25 of your closest running friends a direct message:

  > *"Hey — I've been building a running coaching app for people chasing real times. I've got it in early beta and would love you specifically to try it. Zero obligation to post about it, but if you do use it for your next month of training, any feedback would mean a lot. Lifetime free. Want me to send you a TestFlight link?"*

  Expected conversion: 10–15 installs. These are your founding users.
- **Write the founder's "why I built this" post.** 500 words. Honest. *"I'm a BQ chaser, I got tired of coaches that don't actually read my data, so I built this."* Not marketing language. First-person. Save it as a draft.
- **Record a 60-second phone-camera reel.** You running, you talking. Low production. Authenticity > polish.
- **Create the Strava club.** Zero cost, compounding returns.
- **Set up a simple email capture** — a one-page site at `postrundrip.com` or similar. ConvertKit or MailerLite free tier. Goal for the week: collect 50 emails from personal network.

### Week 2 — Warm up + race-week presence

- **Personal DM round 2** — friends-of-friends your first 15 beta users surfaced. *"Hey, [mutual friend] mentioned you're training for [race]. Just launched this, would love you to try it."*
- **Heat Check Mile race weekend (May 2)** — the app is already in 40+ beta users' hands. The race becomes a natural conversation starter. You're not marketing; you're just showing up with the app people already have.
- **Race-day content (light, authentic)** — iPhone video, founder's IG story sequence, race recap thread on Strava. No paid amplification. The content just exists as a proof point.
- **1 Reddit r/AdvancedRunning post after race** — *"Year 2 of our Austin road mile — I also built a coaching app for people like us, here's what I learned watching 400 runners use it."* Genuine post; zero self-promotion energy. Link in comments if asked.

## 5. Launch week — May 4–10

**This is the coordinated drop.** Everything converges.

- **Monday:** Public founder's post goes live on your personal IG, LinkedIn, Strava. Pinned. "Today's the day."
- **Monday:** Email your list (should be 75–150 by now) with a direct download link. Subject: *"The thing I've been building is ready."*
- **Tuesday:** Reach out to 3 running podcasts with a specific story angle. Not "please feature my app" — *"I've been building a running coach for BQ chasers and learned some surprising things about what AI still gets wrong in coaching — interested in talking about it?"* That angle gets bookings. "Here's my app" doesn't.
- **Tuesday:** Ask 3–5 beta users from your closest circle to post their own experience on their IG / Strava. Not a scripted promo — just *"hey, would you mind sharing what you think, in your own words?"* Genuine posts from trusted voices beat any ad.
- **Wednesday:** Post to r/AdvancedRunning and r/Marathon_Training. Not a launch post. A *content* post — e.g., "I just shipped a weather-adjusted pace calculator for my running coaching app, here's how it works and the formula we used." Give value, mention the app at the end. r/AR smells marketing from three miles away; genuine technical content does great.
- **Thursday:** Reach out to 3 Austin-area running creators with 5–20K followers. Offer free lifetime access. No post required. Most will post anyway; the ones who do will outperform any paid creator deal.
- **Friday:** Run a "coach Q&A" IG Live — 20 minutes, answer training questions live using the app. Low-stakes, high-trust, unique format. Repurpose clips for the next two weeks of content.
- **Weekend:** Batch-create next 3 weeks of content. Founder long run + vlog, app screenshot carousel, blog post draft.

## 6. Post-launch — 6 weeks (May 11 – June 30)

Goal: sustain momentum, compound the network effect, let word-of-mouth do the work.

**Week 3–4:**
- Ship one blog post per week. SEO plus shareability. Topics that ride your differentiation:
  - *"The weather-adjusted pace story"* — you already have the science.
  - *"What an honest AI coach looks like"* — the glass-box philosophy.
  - *"We deleted our own race-history feature. Here's why."* — contrarian, runner-credibility-building.
- Daily Instagram for 14 days straight, then drop to 4x/week. Batch-create every Sunday.
- Track which early users are engaging hardest. DM each one personally after a great workout. *"Saw you crushed that tempo — how's the app working for you? Anything you wish it did better?"* Product feedback + relationship investment in one motion.

**Week 5–6:**
- Pitch 5 more podcasts. By now you have 2+ months of user stories. The pitch gets better: *"I've got 300 runners using my coach, here's what they're teaching me."*
- Earned local-press angle: *"Austin runner builds AI coach that actually passes the Rogue Running test."* Pitch Austin running blogs (not Austin Chronicle, too broad — think local running community blogs, subreddits, newsletter writers). They run these stories.
- **Referral mechanic (cheap, works).** Add a "give a friend a free month, get a free month" loop in-app. Serious runners are in group chats with other serious runners. This is the channel.

**Week 7–8:**
- First cohort review. Of your first 300 users, who's still active at 30 days? Use that data to refine messaging — if X% of retained users are training for a marathon, lean into that.
- **Repeat the DM pattern** with your extended network. The people who were too busy to respond in April might have bandwidth in June. Don't chase; re-offer once.
- Open-source or blog about one technical decision that's interesting — e.g., *"The Postgres trigger we wrote to keep our athlete state fresh"*. Developer-runner Venn diagram is small but passionate; you'll get GitHub stars, a Hacker News hit if you're lucky, and earned backlinks that compound for SEO.

## 7. Channel-by-channel playbook — all zero budget

| Channel | Tactic | Why it works for you |
|---|---|---|
| **Personal DMs** | 30–50 individually tailored messages to your running network | Your single highest-conversion channel. Don't automate. |
| **Friend-of-friend intros** | Ask each beta user: "who should I reach out to next?" | Every user becomes a distribution node |
| **Strava club** | Post your runs with app commentary, engage with members' kudos | Running's actual social network. Free. Compounds. |
| **Instagram organic** | Founder voice, low-fi, runner-to-runner | Free if you're the face. Don't outsource to a marketer — destroys authenticity. |
| **Podcasts (earned)** | Pitch with a specific angle, not a product demo | Pure relationship + story. Free. Reach is enormous for niche audiences. |
| **Running club Q&As** | Ask to come speak at 3–5 Austin run clubs. Free pizza + app invites | Clubs love speakers. You've got a story. |
| **Reddit r/AdvancedRunning** | Contribute genuine content monthly, don't promote | rAR will crucify marketers. They love builders who share work. |
| **Blog + SEO** | One 800-word post per week on training science | Compounds for years. You're already writing docs anyway. |
| **Email newsletter** | Monthly, one piece of real training value + app news | Free tier at MailerLite/ConvertKit. Retention tool. |
| **Heat Check Mile** | Natural amplification moment, not a campaign centerpiece | Race builds audience incidentally. |

That's it. You do not need paid ads at this stage. Paid acquisition for a product you haven't validated is lighting money on fire; paid ads for a BQ-audience product without a clear narrative is worse than that.

## 9. What NOT to do

- **Don't buy Instagram ads.** Targeting "runners in Austin who like running" is the worst CPM category in paid social. Ignore it.
- **Don't run Google search ads for "running coach" or "training plan."** Runna, Nike, and every training app is bidding $5+ CPC for those terms. You'll spend $500 getting 20 clicks and 0 installs.
- **Don't hire a PR firm.** $3–8K/month for earned media you can get with a well-written pitch email. You're better at that than they are.
- **Don't do sponsored-post deals with micro-influencers.** $300 to someone with 15K followers typically yields 3 app installs. Gift them lifetime access for free instead; that's what actually works.
- **Don't do a press release.** Nobody reads them. If you want local running press, email the writer directly with a personal story, not a release.
- **Don't pay for badges or awards** ("Best running app of 2026" listings). Pay-to-play directories are a scam tax on early-stage founders.
- **Don't put out a Product Hunt launch unless you know the Product Hunt game well.** Most PH launches flop and the energy required is high. Skip unless it's a warm network play there too.

## 10. The 5 things to do this week

1. **Build the 50-name network list.** Spreadsheet. Name, relationship, ask. 2 hours.
2. **Send 15 closed-beta DMs** to your closest running friends. Personalized, not copy-paste. 90 minutes.
3. **Write the founder's "why I built this" post.** 500 words, honest, draft-and-sit-on-it-for-a-day. 90 minutes.
4. **Set up the email list + domain.** MailerLite free tier + simple landing page. 60 minutes.
5. **Create the Strava club.** Post your Tuesday run. 20 minutes.

That's ~6 hours total. Everything else flows from those five moves.

## 11. Success criteria — 60 days from launch week

- **300–500 installs**, 60%+ from warm DMs, friend-of-friend, and earned podcast/IG mentions.
- **30-day retention** ≥ 40% (for an app like this, that's a meaningful signal).
- **5 earned podcast or creator features.**
- **20+ organic user IG posts** mentioning or tagging the app.
- **1,000+ Instagram followers** from baseline (organic only).
- **Zero paid advertising spend.**

If you hit those numbers, you've proven the product-distribution fit and can consider actually buying ads to accelerate. If you don't, the answer isn't "spend more on ads" — it's "why didn't the network convert better?" which is a product question, not a marketing question.

---

## 12. The honest truth about connections-based launches

It works when you've built real relationships. It fails when you try to *use* people rather than share something you're proud of. The difference is tone.

Wrong: *"Hey, launching my app — please post about it?"*
Right: *"Built this thing, would love you specifically to try it for your training — zero obligation."*

The first is a favor ask that puts the recipient in an awkward spot. The second is sharing something you made with someone you respect. Serious runners smell the difference immediately.

If your first 50 DMs sound like the first version, stop and redraft. If they sound like the second, send them, and expect the numbers in §11 to hit themselves.
